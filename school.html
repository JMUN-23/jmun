About Seth.M.R.Jaipuria School

Seth MR. Jaipuria School, Gomti Nagar, Lucknow— the School of Conscious Transformation, has been established in the memory of Late Shri Seth Mungturam Jaipuria, a great nationalist and doyen of the Textile Industry, who was conferred the Padma Bhushan by the President of India in 1971 in recognition of his social services.

The school's focus is to provide a learning environment that protects and nourishes three precious qualities inherent in children— innocence, innagination, and a spirit of exploration.

Under the able patronship of Shri Sharad Jaipuria and Smt. Anjali Jaipuria, and imaginative limits and working on novel concepts to make this conference the experience it always is.

This conference has been home to never—seen—before committees passed down as legacy since its inception.

The goal this year, for its eleventh edition, is to make it bigger and better than it has ever been.

Message from the Vice Chairperson

Mrs. Anjali Jaipuria

While the world pats itself on the back for becoming one global family, the truth is that we still have a long way to go to actually forge that bond. Globalization has surely helped us achieve tremendous progress, but somewhere in the process, the essence of belonging to each other has evaporated. We seem to be bound to each other not by a Universal Spirit but the very things that cause irreversible harm to it.

While nations increase the strength of their armies, they hardly have the courage to hold an open and truthful discussion with each other, while we talk of being a global citizen, we find it difficult to accept and transform our own flaws. Terrorism has become a problem which seems to have no visible solution in the near future. In a race to outdo each other, the factor of Human Unity is completely lost and it is only a radical change in human consciousness that can alleviate our plight.

Sri Aurobindo had said, “Nothing but a radical change of consciousness can save humanity from the terrible plight into which it is plunged.” These words, spoken in another era for another generation, hold as much value and relevance today as they did then.

This radical change of consciousness can happen only if young minds imbibe values of Human Unity through a gentle but conscious transformation of their being, by focusing as much on spiritual education as on academic excellence. It is only when the youth become conscious individuals, conscious not only of their outer progress but also conscious of their inner progress, that they will take steps to promote harmony, peace and brotherhood in the community at large.

It is only a conscious transformation of the emotional and mental nature of young adults which will make them more aware of their responsibility, responsibility not only towards their own advancement but a mindful responsibility towards Mother Nature, towards humanity at large. Such inspired minds will take it upon themselves to foster the spirit of Human Unity among all, or what is known as Vasudhaiva Kutumbakum, the whole world is one family.

Educational institutions need to go beyond conventional teaching and create curriculums which foster, through the component of spiritual education, the spirit of Human Unity in their students. And this must be done gradually, through a well curated educational programme, from Playgroup to Grade 12.
This is where exercises like the Model United Nations also come into play. They not only educate students on the delicate socio-political balance of nations, but also help them understand the bigger picture, the universal spirit of equality, fraternity and brotherhood.

I invite students of different schools to participate in Jaipuria MUN heartily. Together may you all raise the consciousness of the Universe with your good will!

Warm Regards
Anjali Jaipuria
Vice Chairperson
SMRJ Gomti Nagar.
Message from the Principal

Mrs. Promini Chopra

If we wish to celebrate life, we must find peace not by avoiding life or trying to escape our problems but by confronting them head on.
Disagreements and conflicts are not worrisome but not finding a solution to them which safeguards all interests is indeed not in the interest of the Globe. Solutions are learnt through experiences and understanding multiple perspectives. Resolutions should emphasise on such inclusive development which boosts economy while being conscious of the responsibility towards both man and nature, This year India, as a host of G 20,will focus on the theme: ‘One Earth, One Family, One Future’. While formulating the G-20 agenda, India has kept in mind the United Nation’s commitment to raise awareness and responsibility towards the Global Issues of climate change, women empowerment, zero hunger, sustainable development.

In tandem with G 20 Summit, JMUN 2023 will also highlight similar issues. It is imperative for children to understand that issues in different regions may be different. Children may be inclined towards the ones which affect them but the Earth and Globe are one so every issue is significant for Global Citizens.
The period of Gupta rule between 300 and 600 CE in India was a period of peace in which trade thrived. It was called the Golden Age of India for its advances in science and emphasis on classical Indian art and literature. Over the centuries, we have known countries whose economy, development and culture touched an abysmal low with no hopes of revival because strife, lack of a larger purpose and conflict within and outside their borders controlled them.

If History is a testimony to Peace being the most dependable associate of development, it is of paramount significance that we give children an experience in sweetening the sting of dissent and political interests by wading through debate, discussion and deliberation to understand the process of peaceful conflict resolution. JMUN 2023, from 27th to 29th July wishes the delegates to have an experience that the final copy of a concrete, sustainable, inclusive development plan will emerge only after all disagreements are settled peacefully with the spirit of ‘Vasudhaiv Kutumbakam’ permeating the conference in letter and spirit.
We look forward to hosting the team from your esteemed institution and learning from the delegates.



Warm Regards
Promini Chopra
Principal
SMRJ Gomti Nagar.
Message from the MUN Coordinator

Dr. Preeti Singh

Ten successful years have passed and now 11th edition of JMUN has finally arrived to carry forward the legacy of past years with the sole aim of awakening consciousness about global politics along with the skills to deal effectively with it. Our resolve to cultivate debates that fully elicit students as Delegates’ skills has met with success through our endeavours. Each year has surprised us with the novelty that was created through agendas on challenging global issues where our young Delegates try to defend their stance and question the missing dots in the policy making.
As Dr. Kalam said: True Knowledge = Creativity + Righteousness + Courage, so problems are common but attitude makes the difference. Let’s encourage our students to ask questions, to deliberate, defend and deliver.

As young individuals who are shaping the future, it is crucial that students play an active role in understanding and addressing the challenges facing our world. Participation will not only expand their knowledge and awareness but also contribute to the development of their critical thinking and problem-solving skills. Moreover, engaging in such discussions instils in them a sense of responsibility towards the world and its people.

As delegates when these students will explore the complexities of global issues, they will learn to view the world from diverse perspectives and understand the interdependence of various countries and societies. This exposure and understanding, in turn, can help them become responsible citizens who are committed to making the world a better place.

11th Edition of JMUN has picked up the essence of world unity and peace together through a platform where simulation of UN, a platform of international cooperation will work as an important committee along with Lok Sabha as a Representative body of People from the World’s largest Democracy –India. This year we have also included G-20 which is working towards creating ‘One Earth, One Family, One Future’. JMUN 2023 is completely aligned with all three platforms which will change the world for better. The discussions will not only yield unique solutions but also further enlighten the participants about the delicate texture of the world body which, many opine, is under serious threat.

Background guides, speeches, position-papers, organisation, deliberation, consideration, lobbying, working papers, resolutions are few of the various terms typical to MUN that make our pulses rise with excitement and anticipation. Imagine awe mingling with doubt, stress merging into confidence and competition growing into unity. There, you have the jar of emotions you’ll be given to handle with.
Looking forward to meet Heads of schools, Faculty in charges and Delegates in July from 27 to 29, 2023 during the 11th session of JMUN.



Regards,
Dr. Preeti Singh
MUN Incharge
SMRJ Gomti Nagar.
