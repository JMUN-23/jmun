About Seth.M.R.Jaipuria School
Seth M.R. Jaipuria School, Gomti Nagar, Lucknow began its journey in 1992. It is recognized as one of the creme-de-la-creme names in education in India which provides education from play group to grade 12. We are guided by the Philosophy of Education promulgated by Sri Aurobindo and The Mother. We believe in partnership with our parents to impart value based education to our children that would chisel them into good human beings, for education is not about getting 90% alone; it is about believing in and living the spirit of "Vasudhaiva Kutumbakam".

The school believes that all education is within so children are exposed to a wide range of co-curricular and extra-curricular activities to find their interests and perfection within. To develop the 21st century skills of analysis, innovation, creativity, problem solving, collaboration and communication, the school encourages student led initiatives.
Some of the student led initiatives are Techvista, Elixir,Akshar Parva, Spoken word poetry, Book Forum, Creative Collaboration Club, Anandotsav, Mridang, Jhankar Beatz and JMUN.
The interschool student led initiatives have found recognition among prominent schools of the country and are much awaited events.

To give you an idea of the range of achievements in the last few years for the school
1) IMRB ranks the school as the 9th Best School in India.
2) Education World has been ranking the school as the 2nd Best School in Uttar Pradesh since 2013.
3) Our students have shown avant-garde results in all fields. In the Board exams 90% of our students score above 85% + marks. Since 2015 every year our students have been there securing a position in top 15 students in the CISCE merit list .
4) Our school has had the honour of winning a the Bronze Medal at the National Geographic World Championship at St. Petersburg, Russia.
5) In the SEARCC Internationals school software competition ‘2016 three of our Class XII students were the Champions after defeating the teams from Australia and Republic of China, Diversity an Art work was acclaimed by the High Commission of Canada in 2017.
6) At Celesta International organised by CMS, we have been acclaimed the best team and were awarded the first prize twice among 67 participating countries.
We have won trophies at the International dance festival Udbhav Utsav several times. In the under 14 category, we were first runner up, our cricket Academy team defeated teams from South African clubs in the India South Africa Cricket Academy's Cup organised by Madan Lal Academy.
7) We are the only school with a 200 piece orchestra which is applauded for its performance all over the city and has also been acclaimed at Inter National events.
8) In sports our students have won acclaim at the national level in Athletics, Badminton, Table Tennis, Cricket, Karate and Hockey.
Our school students have won acclaim in interschool online dance,music, creative writing and debating events conducted by schools across the country. One of our students won the best speaker award in the interschool online Claude Martin Debate.

In the online competitions we won
1) a runner up team trophy among 33 Jaipuria schools from across the state in cultural and literary festival hosted by Jaipuria Schools.
2) a runner up team trophy among 25 prestigious schools of Lucknow in cultural and literary festival Kaleidoscope for Juniors, Aksharparva and Elixir hosted by Seth M.R.Jaipuria School Lucknow.
3) 1st prize among 14 schools of Lucknow in Sanskrit Bhawani Bharti competition.
4) The school has a broad vision and strengthens the children emotionally by organizing spiritual camps and Geeta sessions. Self-defense training classes for students are a regular feature of the school. In the online mode too one of our students became the brand ambassador to arouse gender sensitivity.
5) Our pass outs are studying in prestigious institutions abroad and in in India like SRCC, LSR, Hindu, KGMU, GSVM, IIT’s, IIM’s and Ashoka University. Some of them have been awarded full scholarships due to their academic acumen.
Our alumni are working in India and abroad at prominent positions. They visit the school regularly and uphold the values they have imbibed at school. Alumni conducts online career counselling for ISC students.
6) Our students have won acclaim for themselves in Art, Drama and debating besides maintaining a good academic record.
Message from the Co-Chairperson

Mrs. Anjali Jaipuria

If we wish to celebrate life, we must find peace not by avoiding life or trying to escape our problems but by confronting them head on. Disagreements and conflicts are not worrisome but not finding a solution to them which safeguards all interests is indeed not in the interest of the Globe. Solutions are learnt through experiences and understanding multiple perspectives. Resolutions should emphasise on such inclusive development which boosts economy while being conscious of the responsibility towards both man and nature, This year India, as a host of G 20,will focus on the theme: ‘One Earth, One Family, One Future’. While formulating the G-20 agenda, India has kept in mind the United Nation’s commitment to raise awareness and responsibility towards the Global Issues of climate change, women empowerment, zero hunger, sustainable development. In tandem with G 20 Summit, JMUN 2023 will also highlight similar issues. It is imperative for children to understand that issues in different regions may be different. Children may be inclined towards the ones which affect them but the Earth and Globe are one so every issue is significant for Global Citizens. The period of Gupta rule between 300 and 600 CE in India was a period of peace in which trade thrived. It was called the Golden Age of India for its advances in science and emphasis on classical Indian art and literature. Over the centuries, we have known countries whose economy, development and culture touched an abysmal low with no hopes of revival because strife, lack of a larger purpose and conflict within and outside their borders controlled them. If History is a testimony to Peace being the most dependable associate of development, it is of paramount significance that we give children an experience in sweetening the sting of dissent and political interests by wading through debate, discussion and deliberation to understand the process of peaceful conflict resolution. JMUN 2023, from 27th to 29th July wishes the delegates to have an experience that the final copy of a concrete, sustainable, inclusive development plan will emerge only after all disagreements are settled peacefully with the spirit of ‘Vasudhaiv Kutumbakam’ permeating the conference in letter and spirit. We look forward to hosting the team from your esteemed institution and learning from the delegates. Warm Regards Promini Chopra Principal SMRJ Gomti Nagar. .

Regards,
Promini Chopra
Principal

Message from the MUN Coordinator

Dr. Preeti Singh

Ten successful years have passed and now 11th edition of JMUN has finally arrived to carry forward the legacy of past years with the sole aim of awakening consciousness about global politics along with the skills to deal effectively with it. Our resolve to cultivate debates that fully elicit students as Delegates’ skills has met with success through our endeavours. Each year has surprised us with the novelty that was created through agendas on challenging global issues where our young Delegates try to defend their stance and question the missing dots in the policy making. As Dr. Kalam said: True Knowledge = Creativity + Righteousness + Courage, so problems are common but attitude makes the difference. Let’s encourage our students to ask questions, to deliberate, defend and deliver. As young individuals who are shaping the future, it is crucial that students play an active role in understanding and addressing the challenges facing our world. Participation will not only expand their knowledge and awareness but also contribute to the development of their critical thinking and problem-solving skills. Moreover, engaging in such discussions instils in them a sense of responsibility towards the world and its people. As delegates when these students will explore the complexities of global issues, they will learn to view the world from diverse perspectives and understand the interdependence of various countries and societies. This exposure and understanding, in turn, can help them become responsible citizens who are committed to making the world a better place. 11th Edition of JMUN has picked up the essence of world unity and peace together through a platform where simulation of UN, a platform of international cooperation will work as an important committee along with Lok Sabha as a Representative body of People from the World’s largest Democracy –India. This year we have also included G-20 which is working towards creating ‘One Earth, One Family, One Future’. JMUN 2023 is completely aligned with all three platforms which will change the world for better. The discussions will not only yield unique solutions but also further enlighten the participants about the delicate texture of the world body which, many opine, is under serious threat. Background guides, speeches, position-papers, organisation, deliberation, consideration, lobbying, working papers, resolutions are few of the various terms typical to MUN that make our pulses rise with excitement and anticipation. Imagine awe mingling with doubt, stress merging into confidence and competition growing into unity. There, you have the jar of emotions you’ll be given to handle with. Looking forward to meet Heads of schools, Faculty in charges and Delegates in July from 27 to 29, 2023 during the 11th session of JMUN.

Regards,
Dr. Preeti Singh
MUN In Charge
Seth M.R. Jaipuria School, Gomtinagar, Lucknow.
