About Seth.M.R.Jaipuria School
Seth M.R. Jaipuria School, Gomti Nagar, Lucknow began its journey in 1992. It is recognized as one of the creme-de-la-creme names in education in India which provides education from play group to grade 12. We are guided by the Philosophy of Education promulgated by Sri Aurobindo and The Mother. We believe in partnership with our parents to impart value based education to our children that would chisel them into good human beings, for education is not about getting 90% alone; it is about believing in and living the spirit of "Vasudhaiva Kutumbakam".

The school believes that all education is within so children are exposed to a wide range of co-curricular and extra-curricular activities to find their interests and perfection within. To develop the 21st century skills of analysis, innovation, creativity, problem solving, collaboration and communication, the school encourages student led initiatives.
Some of the student led initiatives are Techvista, Elixir,Akshar Parva, Spoken word poetry, Book Forum, Creative Collaboration Club, Anandotsav, Mridang, Jhankar Beatz and JMUN.
The interschool student led initiatives have found recognition among prominent schools of the country and are much awaited events.

1) IMRB ranks the school as the 9th Best School in India.
2) Education World has been ranking the school as the 2nd Best School in Uttar Pradesh since 2013.
3) Our students have shown avant-garde results in all fields. In the Board exams 90% of our students score above 85% + marks. Since 2015 every year our students have been there securing a position in top 15 students in the CISCE merit list .
4) Our school has had the honour of winning a the Bronze Medal at the National Geographic World Championship at St. Petersburg, Russia.
5) In the SEARCC Internationals school software competition ‘2016 three of our Class XII students were the Champions after defeating the teams from Australia and Republic of China, Diversity an Art work was acclaimed by the High Commission of Canada in 2017.

We have won trophies at the International dance festival Udbhav Utsav several times. In the under 14 category, we were first runner up, our cricket Academy team defeated teams from South African clubs in the India South Africa Cricket Academy's Cup organised by Madan Lal Academy.
7) We are the only school with a 200 piece orchestra which is applauded for its performance all over the city and has also been acclaimed at Inter National events.
8) In sports our students have won acclaim at the national level in Athletics, Badminton, Table Tennis, Cricket, Karate and Hockey.
Our school students have won acclaim in interschool online dance,music, creative writing and debating events conducted by schools across the country. One of our students won the best speaker award in the interschool online Claude Martin Debate.

Message from the Principal

Mrs. Promini Chopra

Being the simplest, thinking with words is limiting, higher than this is thinking with ideas but the highest is thinking with experiences. So, we bring to you the 2022 ‘in school space’ inexhaustible JMUN thinking experience after a skip in 2020 and the online experience in 2021.
More often than not, we study under duress of the conventional responsibility of a student to memorise information, do well in a testing system by becoming adept at answering anticipated questions, so that the ultimate objective of a secure profession and a good position in the society is achieved. JMUN ushers in an opportunity to study about world affairs past and present not to fulfil this conventional responsibility but to do what you should do, to be what you should be so that you usher in decisive and true progress.
I look forward to all students coming together to enliven JMUN by stepping into different roles not merely in the visible demesne but to augment their zenith in skills that life demands for excellence. Information, analysis, ruminations, discussions, debates before deliverance are skills that you need to lead a fulfilling life provided you keep a watchful eye on your intent in presenting your opinion.
As you conduct your role in the eight well chartered committees, ‘Be Reverent Of All points of View’ by putting each one in its true place without insistence on some to the exclusion of others. Progress and the service of truth are based on harmony and not division. You will achieve this larger purpose only if you consciously lead yourself to a deeper understanding and the widening of your spirit by abolition of these limits of the self.
Heartfelt Good Wishes for JMUN 2022 With the aspiration that in JMUN 2022, the Hall Mark will be development of personal attributes that promote healing, compassion, honesty, and integrity along with enthusiastic learning and an uncompromising quest for excellence.

Regards,
Promini Chopra
Principal
Message from the MUN Coordinator

Dr. Preeti Singh

In the words of The Mother, “The more a mind is ignorant, the more easily it judges everything it does not know or is incapable of understanding.” The aim is to rid this ignorance and grow as responsible individuals that are an asset to society and the growing world at large.
The world today is in crisis. There is no putting an end to organised crime, need for world peace and sustainable development. If anything, these times have shown us the need for peace and diplomacy using dialogue. The world has become a global village and no age is too young to prepare for the future.
For school students, a Model United Nations conference serves as an ideal stepping-stone into the global arena.
At Jaipuria Model United Nations, we aim to empower. We aim to grow and we aim to preach what we intend to practise.
The United Nations Model Programme at Jaipuria School aims to build and maintain strong links between students across the globe. We teach students how to make their simulations more accurate, and share knowledge of what the actual UN is like; encourage them to take real action to support UN values and the Sustainable Development Goals.
In the classroom, students learn from their teachers. At conferences, students learn from each other. The conference does not replace the classroom; the conference complements the classroom. Students have to internalise what they learn in class and deliver that information through speeches, caucusing, and resolutions.
Educational institutions need to go beyond conventional teaching and create curriculums that foster, through the component of spiritual education, the spirit of humanity in their students. I heartily invite all of you to join us as we commemorate the Tenth Edition of Jaipuria Model United Nations.

Regards,
Dr. Preeti Singh
