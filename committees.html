Committees

United Nations General Assembly (UNGA)

Agenda : Deliberating upon the threat of Missile Technologies with special emphasis on Ballistic Missiles and Anti-Missile Defense Systems

The accelerated proliferation of missiles has cornered the political leaders into a witness box. Under the guise of building protective armaments, States continue to accumulate weapons of mass destruction. The Hague Code of Conduct serves as a mere political agreement encouraging States to annually report their activities related to missiles. The code fails to legally bind its signatories and keep a check on them. In all probability, the demise of global security is just a push of a button away. The advancement of missile technologies increases the chances of destruction by tenfold in the fragile political order that is hanging by a thread. This year, we invite the delegates to Deliberate, Defend and Deliver over the increasing proliferation of armaments and blurring lines between offense and defense.

United Nations Security Council (UNSC)

Agenda : Tackling the threats to Peace and Security in the Balkans in light of the Srpskan Revolution of 2025

The cross swords between East and West are only strengthened with the passage of time. To stabilise its own, any state’s prestige is put for show in the popular name of ‘collateral damage.’ The Balkan States are old puppets in this mutiny between East and West and therefore, the hope of the United Nations Security Council to be simulated at the Tenth Edition of Jaipuria Model United Nations is that the year 2025 shall be the senectitude of the Balkan-misery at the hands of East and West. A politico-economic crisis in 1991-92 disintegrated Yugoslav beheld to intercept any crisis in the volatile region of Eastern Europe, having failed to prevent the annihilation of Ukraine by Russian Forces in the year of 2022. Ladies and Gentlemen, Serbia, inspired by the Russian Federation, is most resolute on broadening its borders by merging with itself, Republika Srpska. By reason of defective governmental setup, there has been a likewise Sprskan demand to secede from Bosnia and Herzegovina and declare independence or unite with Serbia. Will this be another show of power politics or this time, the stakes will be different?

G7 ADVISORY COMMITTEE ON DECENTRALISATION

Agenda : Roadmap for the Future of Blockchain Technology

The G7 Advisory Committee comprises government officials and private representatives that have been tasked with providing the world with a Roadmap For the Future of Blockchain Technology. Blockchain tech aims to revolutionize all existing administrative and financial systems and is already starting to gain traction in its nascent stages. However, its potential threats weigh as heavily as its merits. The search for a perennial and substantial power source to facilitate the existence of cryptocurrencies continues with no evident breakthroughs. The shift to a pseudo world, the metaverse, comes with its own implications and the authenticity of Decentralized Autonomous Organizations remains under severe scrutiny. This new form of technology, under the patronage of private institutions, runs on the idea of decentralisation which goes against the very concept of governance. The battle to reach a middle ground must continue for the holistic development of this world. Delegates must decide- is blockchain the next step in human evolution or a mere fallacy?

Global Democracy Summit (GDS)

Agenda : Discussing the Problem of Authoritarianism in its Varied Forms with Special Emphasis on Safeguarding Democracy

"The problem to be solved is, not what form of government is perfect, but which of the forms is least imperfect." -James Madison The seeds of democracy were first sown in ancient Greece in the 5th century BCE which bloomed into the Athenian Democracy. For the first time, this despotic world witnessed self-rule and was galvanised into reviewing the socio-political system. Since then, the parameters of democracy have undergone profound changes keeping pace with the ever-changing social, economic and political factors. Two turbulent millennia later,ent nations to their perquisite and each one considers its model to be superior by forcing it on others. The saga of futile recrimination and retaliation by nations subsists while the onerous task of tackling the volatile socio-economic dilemmas falls upon the shoulders of the citizens.

Global Press Summit (GPS)

Agenda : Introduction of Global Media Ethics

Gone are the days when media was viewed as a local craft by the general public. The existing set of ethics is no longer sufficient for directing the difficulties that global journalism encounters today. The fundamental ethical duty of global journalists in times of uncertainty is autonomous, hard-edged reporting backed by investigations and assessment. Given that journalism is becoming increasingly multimodal in style and global in scope and effect, it is crucial that media ethics examine its core principles, norms, and practices from a more cosmopolitan perspective. At the Tenth Edition of JMUN, the Global Press Summit plans on seeing reporters Deliberate, Defend and Deliver from around the world to form a comprehensive and pragmatic Code of Ethics that can be accepted and implemented globally.
